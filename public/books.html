Book Recommendations

A life changing book for me, identifiying problems in my life in excruciating detail while offering logical and practical solutions. This book has perhaps had a more postitive impact on my life than all others combined. I believe that reading this book is required to understand our complex and modern world and how we could fit in it.

Reading this right now. So far, a masterful sequel to the first.

This autobiography gives us great insight into how we can overcome the extreme obstacles positioned throughout our lives. A real story of how your success is primarily determined by how you treat the world, and not how the world treats you.

Wiesel's reflection of his time as a Holocaust prisoner serves as a somber reminder of just how cruel humans can be to each other. This book is a sad journey into the evils of humanity and their sudden capability to manifest. Each person has the power to bring about hell into our reality, so we must wield it most wisely.

This memoir is a personal favorite that is both hilarious and dramatic. I find the stories very relatable, asking if Blanco had the same family as me, and very compelling. I wouldn't say this book is a masterpiece or wholly innovative, but it is a fun ride.

A great textbook on how to engineer software that is both flexible and reliable in avoidance of technical debt through the use of design patterns. It does an amazing job of explaining the motivation for design patterns and how to arrive at them on your own. This book helped me make the transition from writing 5 file Java applications to creating much larger projects by giving me a more thoughtful approach to software design.
